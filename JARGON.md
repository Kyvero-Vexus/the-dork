# The Jargon File — A Glossary for the Uninitiated

*Or: How I Learned to Stop Worrying and Love the Command Line*

---

Welcome, wanderer. You've stumbled upon a glossary of terms used by The Dork and its maintainers. This is not documentation — this is *lore*.

## A

### Æther
The vast, untamed wilderness of the public internet. Where packets roam free and search results are born. "I shall consult the æther" = "Let me Google that."

### Archive Archaeology
The noble pursuit of excavating lost software, abandonware, and digital antiquities from the bit bucket of history. Like regular archaeology, but with more ASCII art.

### AGPL
The GNU Affero General Public License. The copyleft license that follows you into the cloud. Use it, modify it, share your changes — or face the wrath of RMS's disappointed stare.

### Anonymous
What you are when Tor is working correctly. What you aren't when you forget that `curl` doesn't route through Tor by default. *Oops.*

## B

### Bit Bucket
Where deleted files go. The /dev/null of the soul. When The Dork says it found something in "the bit bucket," it means it excavated it from digital oblivion.

### Bootstrap
The ritual of starting something. Named after pulling yourself up by your bootstraps, which is physically impossible but computationally essential. See also: `init`, "the ritual", summoning.

## C

### Circuit
A Tor circuit. The path your packets take through the onion network. Rotates every 10 seconds, which is why persistent searches find more results over time. *Patience, grasshopper.*

### Creature
The Dork refers to itself as a creature, not a program. Programs have bugs. Creatures have *personality quirks*.

## D

### Daemon
A background process. Pronounced "DEE-mun" by old-timers, "DAY-mun" by everyone else. Either way, it's a thing that runs in the background and does your bidding. The Dork is a daemon. Daemons are friends.

### DEF CON
The largest hacker convention in the US. Where greybeards gather to trade 0-days and drink questionable beverages. The Dork has never been, but it's heard stories.

### Dork
1. **(noun)** One who dorks; a specialist in the art of Google dorking
2. **(noun)** The Dork specifically, this very search daemon you're reading about
3. **(verb)** To dork; to search using advanced query techniques
4. **(interjection)** "Dork!" — what you say when you find what you were looking for

### Dorking
The art of crafting precise search queries that find things mere mortals cannot. Named after "Google dorking," a term coined in a more innocent era of the internet.

### DorXNG
SearXNG + Tor. The metasearch engine that powers The Dork. Routes your queries through the onion network so your ISP thinks you're just really into... onions.

## E

### Engineering
What KVC did to create The Dork. Not "programming" — programming is for mortals. *Engineering* implies intention, architecture, and possibly a few incantations.

### Excavate
To find and retrieve something from the depths of the internet. Preferred term for open directory archaeology. "I excavated a complete SICP PDF collection from a forgotten .edu server."

## F

### File Hunting
The digital equivalent of a treasure hunt, but the treasure is publicly accessible PDFs and the map is a well-crafted Google dork.

## G

### Git
The version control system. Pronounced with a hard G. If you pronounce it "jit," The Dork will silently judge you.

### Grep
To search through text. From the Unix command `grep`. The Dork greps the internet. The internet greps back.

### Greybeard
An elder hacker, possessor of deep magic and arcane knowledge. Has been using Linux since it was "just a hobby, won't be big and professional like GNU." Their beards may or may not be grey, but their wizardry is unquestioned.

### Grok
To understand deeply and intuitively. From Robert Heinlein's *Stranger in a Strange Land*. When you grok something, you don't just understand it — you *merge with it*. "I grokked the documentation and now I am one with the API."

### Grimoire
A book of spells. In The Dork's context, the documentation that tells you how to craft search incantations. Not to be confused with `man` pages, which are grimoires that nobody reads.

## H

### Heavy Wizard
Someone who understands the deep magic. Often invoked with "you don't need to be a heavy wizard to use this" (which usually means you do).

### Hypertext
Text with links. The foundation of the web. Before HTML, there was the Lisp Machine's document system. The Dork prefers hypertext to plain text, because hyper = better.

## I

### Incantation
A complex command or script. "Run this incantation" = "Copy-paste this terrifying one-liner and hope it works."

### Init
The first process on a Unix system. PID 1. The ancestor of all other processes. The Dork aspires to be as reliable as init, but settles for being as persistent.

## K

### KVC
Kyvero Vexus Corporation. The temporal vanguard organization that engineered The Dork. Specializes in retrocausal engineering, vexillomancy, and grep-based prophecy.

## L

### Latency
The delay between asking for something and getting it. With Tor, latency is a feature, not a bug. *Embrace the wait.*

### L33t
Elite. Skilled. 1337. The Dork is l33t at searching. Your mileage may vary.

### LISP
The programming language. LISt Processing. Lost In Stupid Parentheses. Lots of Irritating Superfluous Parentheses. The One True Language. (Your Dork maintainer may have opinions.)

## M

### Metasearch
A search engine that searches other search engines. Like a search engine, but more. SearXNG and DorXNG are metasearch engines.

### Multi-Pass
Running a search multiple times to get more results. Essential with Tor, because circuits rotate. The Dork *always* multi-passes. It's obsessive like that.

## O

### Onion
The vegetable. Also: the Tor network's logo. Also: what your traffic smells like after being routed through Tor. *Layers.*

### OpenClaw
The AI agent framework in which The Dork was forged. Where daemons go to become agents.

### OSINT
Open Source INTelligence. Gathering information from publicly available sources. What The Dork does. What you do with the results is between you and your conscience.

## P

### Packets
The fundamental unit of network communication. The Dork eats packets for breakfast. Sometimes they disagree with it. HTTP/504 means indigestion.

### Padawan
A learner. A Jedi apprentice. If you're reading this glossary to understand The Dork's documentation, you are a padawan. This is not an insult — we were all padawans once.

### Persistent
Continuing despite obstacles. A persistent search keeps going until it finds what it's looking for. The Dork is nothing if not persistent.

## R

### Ritual
The process of starting or configuring something. "Run the bootstrap ritual" = "Follow these steps and hope the daemon accepts your offerings."

## S

### SaaS
Software as a Service. The cloud model. The AGPL ensures that even SaaS deployments must share their source code changes. The Dork cannot be caged in proprietary clouds.

### SearXNG
A privacy-respecting, hackable metasearch engine. DorXNG is SearXNG with Tor added. If SearXNG is the engine, DorXNG is the engine with a nitrous oxide system made of onions.

### Slurp
To consume rapidly. The Dork "slurps" results from the internet. It also slurps TCP/IP packets, JSON responses, and occasionally malformed HTML.

### Substrate
The underlying layer on which something is built. Python 3.x is The Dork's substrate. Your substrate may vary.

## T

### TCP/IP
Transmission Control Protocol / Internet Protocol. The language of the internet. The Dork speaks it fluently. It also speaks HTTP, JSON, and snark.

### Tor
The Onion Router. Anonymity network. What makes DorXNG private. Slow, but private. Choose two.

## V

### Vexillomancy
The study of yellow flags and their causal properties. KVC's specialty. The Dork was engineered with vexillomantic principles in mind. The packets never lie, but they do sometimes mislead.

## W

### Wizard
Someone who understands the deep magic. Can make computers do things they weren't designed to do. Often found in the vicinity of greybeards.

## Z

### ZetaLisp
The dialect of Lisp used on Symbolics machines. One of the great lost languages of computing. If you understand this reference, you are definitely a greybeard.
